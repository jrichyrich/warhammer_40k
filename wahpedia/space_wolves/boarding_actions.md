# Boarding Actions: Space Wolves

Boarding Actions are close-quarters engagements in the cramped corridors of space hulks and derelict ships. Space Wolves excel in these environments, where their melee prowess is most effective.

## Army Rule: Oath of Moment
In Boarding Actions, the Space Marine army rule remains **Oath of Moment**. At the start of your Command phase, you select one enemy unit. Until your next Command phase, your models can **re-roll Hit rolls** against that target.

---

## **1. Boarding Strike**
This is the standard detachment for most Space Marine infantry. It focuses on close-quarters firefights and holding corridors.

### **Detachment Rule: Drive Home the Blade**
Each time an **ADEPTUS ASTARTES** unit is selected to shoot, if any models are within 1" of an open hatchway and the target is on the opposite side, that unit's ranged weapons gain **[SUSTAINED HITS 1]**.

### **Stratagems**
*   **Ceramite Bulwark (1 CP):** (Fight phase) One **BATTLELINE** unit gains +1 Attack and +1 Strength if targeting an enemy on the opposite side of a hatchway.
*   **Vox-Amplified Roar (1 CP):** (Charge phase) Force an enemy unit being charged to take a Battle-shock test.
*   **In the Emperor’s Name (1 CP):** (Enemy phase) One **BATTLELINE** unit within range of an objective gains a **5+ Feel No Pain**.

### **Enhancements**
*   **Trademark Weapon:** +1 Strength and +1 Damage to one ranged weapon (excluding Torrent).
*   **Superior Boarding Tactics:** Start the battle with 2 CP instead of 0.
*   **Close Quarters Killer:** Re-roll Wound rolls for melee attacks.
*   **Expert Breacher:** The bearer's unit can operate a hatchway at the start or end of their Move step.

## **2. Pilum Strike Team**
This detachment is designed for high mobility and "hit-and-fade" tactics. It is the primary home for **Hounds of Morkai**.

### **Detachment Rule: Relentless Salvoes**
**ADEPTUS ASTARTES** units are eligible to shoot in a turn in which they **Advanced** or **Fell Back**.

### **Stratagems**
*   **Knife Work (1 CP):** (Fight phase) Improve the Strength and AP of melee weapons by 1.
*   **Master Marksman (1 CP):** (Shooting phase) Ranged weapons gain **[DEVASTATING WOUNDS]**.
*   **Enhanced Efficiency (1 CP):** (Movement phase) After a unit shoots, it can make a Normal Move of up to 6" (cannot end in Engagement Range).

### **Enhancements**
*   **Omnistructural Auspex:** After the bearer shoots an enemy, other friendly units gain **+1 AP** against that target for the rest of the phase.
*   **Void Warrior:** Re-roll Advance and Charge rolls for the bearer; the bearer can charge even if they Advanced.

---

## **3. Terminator Assault**
This detachment represents the elite spearhead of the Chapter, featuring heavy armor and powerful melee options.

### **Detachment Rule: Sacred Oath**
Once per battle, when you select an **Oath of Moment** target, your models can **re-roll Wound rolls** in addition to Hit rolls against that target for that turn. Additionally, **TERMINATOR** units gain the ability to secure objectives as if they were **BATTLELINE**.

### **Stratagems**
*   **Teleport Strike (1 CP):** Allows a unit to Deep Strike within 6" of an enemy (instead of 9"), but they cannot declare a charge that turn.
*   **Fury of the First (1 CP):** (Shooting phase) Add 1 to the Hit roll for a **TERMINATOR** unit.
*   **Point-Blank Overwatch (1 CP):** Allows a unit to use the **Overwatch** stratagem on a **5+** instead of a 6.

### **Enhancements**
*   **Shield of the Imperium:** The bearer gains a **4+ Feel No Pain** against Mortal Wounds.
*   **Warrior of Legend:** The bearer can re-roll one Hit, Wound, or Damage roll per phase.

---

## **Space Wolves Specific Adaptations**
*   **Wolf Guard:** Non-terminator **Wolf Guard** can be taken in the **Boarding Strike** detachment.
*   **Pack Leaders:** You can include **Wolf Guard Pack Leaders** (Terminator or regular) to join their respective squads, providing their unique buffs.
*   **Mustering:** Restricted unit lists apply to each detachment, but Space Wolves retain access to their unique characters and units where they fit the detachment keywords (e.g., **Arjac Rockfist** and **Logan Grimnar** in **Terminator Assault**).
